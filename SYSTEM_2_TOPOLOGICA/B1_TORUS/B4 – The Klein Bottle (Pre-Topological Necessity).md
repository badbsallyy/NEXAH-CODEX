# 🟣 B4 · The Klein Bottle  
## Orientation Collapse Without Boundary

> *The Klein bottle is not a surface we choose.  
It is the surface that appears once orientation can no longer be preserved.*

---

## 📍 Position in the Codex

B4 follows **necessarily** from the previous steps:

| Layer | Role |
|-----:|------|
| GEOMETRIA NOVA | Local Euclidean validity |
| AXIOM 0 | Breakdown of global linearity |
| B1 · Torus | First closed global structure |
| B2 · Möbius Strip | Orientation becomes unstable |
| **B4 · Klein Bottle** | Orientation collapses globally |

The Klein bottle is **not an extension of the torus**,  
and **not a doubled Möbius strip**.

It is the **first structure where orientation ceases to exist globally**.

---

## 🔁 What Changes at B4

Up to this point:

- loops exist  
- closure exists  
- return paths exist  

But orientation could still be *tracked*.

With the Klein bottle:

- every global traversal flips orientation
- there is **no consistent left/right**
- no global normal vector exists
- inside and outside cannot be separated

This is **not a paradox**.  
It is a **structural inevitability**.

---

## 🧠 Core Structural Insight

The Klein bottle is:

- closed
- without boundary
- non-orientable
- not embeddable in ℝ³ without self-intersection

But most importantly:

> **It is the first geometry where orientation is not a property of space.**

Orientation becomes **path-dependent**.
